About BEET
This self assessment Building Energy Efficiency Tool (BEET) is designed to provide individual users a preliminary rapid assessment platform where they can assess their building energy performance. The overall objective of BEET is three fold:
a. Support the development of a rapid assessment of the energy performance of individual buildings;
b. Create a comparative evaluation between the baseline energy performance with energy efficiency (EE) standards.
c. Share recommendations of potential EE measures across building components and systems .
The tool is intended to cater to the needs of municipalities and relevant stakeholders. The tool may be used for overall understanding of impact on overall building energy consumption profile with respect to various system and component level parameters. The target users may be energy managers, analysts, maintenance and operation professionals, technicians and any individual with basic know-how on buildings and energy efficiency.

Disclaimer

The objective of the tool is to provide an indicative and holistic outcome on the building energy efficiency based on the user's input data. The tool does not replace the simulations and assessments from a full fledged tools and licensed software available in the market. The output results hence is recommended to be used for reference purpose only and may not be used for detailed or investment grade design and implementation activities.
The tool is built based on technical literature data, available global and local standards, tool developers' knowledge and experience in the sector. Care has been taken to deliver the most possibly suitable platform. However, due to the challenges in availability of country specific data with respect to climate files, building envelope standards, equipment and appliances, energy efficiency applications, operations, technology and related resources, etc. we understand that there may be certain differences with other similar available online and licensed tools. All the assumptions have been listed in the Assumptions page.

TOOL ASSUMPTIONS

* Locations with hourly climate data available are only considered in the tool for now.
* If the “No, I don’t know” option is selected, appropriate average/efficiency/benchmark values are loaded to the tool based on location, building type and preceding answers in the tool. These values are adopted as applicable from several published and widely referred sources such as Ministry of Science, Technology and Innovation – Argentina, University of La Plata – Argentina, ASHRAE standards, U.S. Energy Information Administration, U.S. Department of Energy, Energy Star ratings – U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, Country Savings assessments – United for Efficiency UNEP, Bureau of Energy Efficiency – India, design details from equipment / appliance manufacturers and suppliers, years of energy auditing experience, etc.
* Building is a 12' floor-to-floor, square footprint
* Respective place weather data from
http://climate.onebuilding.org/WMO_Region_3_South_America/ARG_Argentina/index.html
* Envelope convective heat transfer is not considered
* Envelope solar gain is not considered, except for windows
* The building is treated as a single thermal zone
* Occupied hours first fill weekdays symmetrically around noon, and then fill weekends
Infiltration is assumed zero during occupied times (vac pressurization)
* Airside economizer operates only when full cooling load can be met - no partial economizer with mechanical cooling
* Lights and plug loads are assumed to be at 1/20 power density during unoccupied times
* There is no energy consumption calculated for hot water heating. There is no energy calculated for hot water hydronic pumping
* Air system is in variable volume setting by default unless the user chooses "No" option. * Window area is equally distributed on N,S,E,W exposures. Window to Wall ratio does ratio default inputs does not consider impact on lighting energy.
* Window solar - Normal radiation is based on hourly solar angles
* Indoor air relative humidity maintained at 50%. The temperature is user defined.
* Only average most common efficient values considered for comparison. However, relatively highest and better efficiency values are available in the market. Please reach out to us, if you do have such values. We will integrate the same into the tool.
* At times, average most efficient values are considered for calculation when the user selects option B, i.e. if the user does not know the value.
* Section 2.4 Occupant density Option C: Max occupant density value a month the selected building category/spaces is considered.
* Section 3.2 R value for Roofs: Both winter (inv) and summer (ver) values are given. However the higher U values i.e. for summer are being considered for calculations for Option B, Option C selection.
* Section 5.1: Option B, standard average efficiencies are considered. Also, for electrical radiators, only equipment efficiency is considered as 100% since entire electricity input is converted to heat energy. However its real usage energy efficiency may be lower if other grid losses are considered.
* Section 6.1: Operating hours of appliances/equipment assumed to be at least 50% of daily occupancy hours in case the user does not mention the actual operating hours in Input_Plugloads1.1 Sheet.
